Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: December 2024

Important Medical Information

Please read this disclaimer carefully before using Risa Medical services.

1. Professional Medical Advice

The information provided on this website and during consultations is for general informational purposes and does not constitute medical advice for specific individuals. Always seek professional medical advice from qualified healthcare providers for your particular health conditions.

2. Emergency Medical Situations

⚠️ MEDICAL EMERGENCIES

If you are experiencing a medical emergency, do not use this website or wait for a consultation. Call 999 immediately or go to your nearest Accident & Emergency department.

Emergency situations include but are not limited to:

  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe allergic reactions
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Severe bleeding or trauma
  • Signs of stroke or heart attack
  • Severe mental health crisis or suicidal thoughts

3. Limitation of Online Information

The content on this website:

  • Is not a substitute for professional medical diagnosis, advice, or treatment
  • May not reflect the most current medical developments
  • Is intended for UK residents and may not apply to other jurisdictions
  • Should not be used to self-diagnose or self-treat

4. Consultation Limitations

In-Person Consultations:

While we strive to provide comprehensive care, some conditions may require:

  • Referral to specialists
  • Hospital-based investigations
  • Emergency medical intervention
  • Ongoing NHS care

Telehealth Consultations:

Video/phone consultations have additional limitations:

  • Limited physical examination capabilities
  • Technology may affect communication quality
  • Not suitable for all medical conditions
  • May require follow-up in-person consultation

5. Prescription Medications

Important information about prescriptions:

  • All prescriptions are issued based on clinical assessment
  • Follow dosage instructions exactly as prescribed
  • Report any adverse reactions immediately
  • Do not share prescription medications
  • Inform us of all other medications you are taking
  • Some medications require regular monitoring

6. Treatment Outcomes

Please understand that:

  • No medical treatment is 100% guaranteed to be successful
  • Individual responses to treatment vary
  • Some conditions may require multiple treatment approaches
  • Side effects or complications may occur despite best practice
  • Regular follow-up may be necessary

7. Patient Responsibilities

For your safety and optimal care:

  • Provide accurate and complete medical history
  • Report all current medications and supplements
  • Follow treatment plans as prescribed
  • Attend follow-up appointments as recommended
  • Report any concerning symptoms promptly
  • Seek emergency care when appropriate

8. Second Opinions

You are always entitled to seek a second opinion from another qualified healthcare provider. We encourage this for:

  • Complex or serious diagnoses
  • Major treatment decisions
  • When you have concerns about recommended treatment
  • Chronic or persistent conditions

9. Regulatory Compliance

Risa Medical operates under:

  • General Medical Council (GMC) regulation
  • Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) guidelines
  • Care Quality Commission (CQC) standards
  • UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018
  • Professional indemnity insurance requirements

10. Complaints and Concerns

If you have concerns about your care:

  • Contact us directly: complaints@risamedical.co.uk
  • Contact the GMC for serious professional concerns
  • Contact your local health authority if needed
  • Seek immediate medical attention if your condition worsens

11. Consent

By using our services, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to this medical disclaimer. You understand the limitations of our services and your responsibilities as a patient.
